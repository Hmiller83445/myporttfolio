Spaghetti Carbonara

History of the Dish

Spaghetti Carbonara is a traditional Italian pasta dish that originated in Rome. It became popular in the mid-20th century and is known for its creamy sauce made from eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. Many believe the dish was created for Italian charcoal workers, which is why the name may come from the word “carbonaro,” meaning “charcoal burner.” Learn more at this source.

Recipie

Description

This recipe creates a rich and flavorful bowl of classic spaghetti carbonara using simple ingredients. The dish is creamy, savory, and comforting without using any actual cream.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Pancetta or guanciale
  • Eggs
  • Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Black pepper
  • Salt

Directions

  1. Boil spaghetti until al dente.
  2. Cook pancetta in a pan until crispy.
  3. Beat eggs and mix with grated cheese.
  4. Combine hot pasta with pancetta.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in the egg-cheese mixture.
  6. Season with black pepper and serve.
